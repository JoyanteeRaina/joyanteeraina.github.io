The Other

Most of the narratives of and from the queer community are the stories of oppression and discrimination. But has it always been like this? Looking backward from the current discourses on the queer community, we find something very opposite to what it is now — the subcontinent as one of the most accepting of diversity.

And then the British came to rule. Queer communities became the reason for colonial anxiety because they were ungovernable. They introduced Section 377 and criminalized them. Even long after the British period, that law, that perspective still exists with all of its statuses.

I tried to find an alternative narrative of the queer community from the reference of Hindu mythology to show the transition that happened without any reasonable basis. To me, this is neither sacred nor taboo, neither pride nor shame. This is exactly what it is.
