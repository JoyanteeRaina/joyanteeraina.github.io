03 / 04 · Bangladesh

White Wounds

Along with climate change, shrimp cultivation in the coastal area of Bangladesh made this region unbearably salty. The water carries the salt, and the soil holds it. The area, which was once full of life, now has neither crops, nor birds or cattle.

The effect of salt reached the uterus of women, who had to cut off this significant body part because of infection, tumors, or cancer. The number of separations is higher than in any other area of Bangladesh. Many men left the region, either for better work opportunities or for a better life. This whole condition has created a different kind of family structure where women are more visible. They share the same wounds and carry the same scars as nature does.

Still, I didn't see any sadness in their eyes. There is no endless waiting for good days to come. Only the continuous struggle of nature and women for survival.
