02 / 04 · Nepal

From Everywhere
and Nowhere

The two main aspects of the Raute life are to never settle down and never do farming. With less than 150 members, the last nomadic group of Nepal has made the forest their home, where the line between public and private life becomes transparent.

Their life revolves in a loop of creating and breaking down their own houses and impermanence is the only constant. However, the constantly shifting cultural landscape and the pressure from the outside world to settle down have created a bicultural identity for them. Still, with a strong sense of identity, they're trying their best to preserve their cultural autonomy.
